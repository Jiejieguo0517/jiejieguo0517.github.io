Neural Speech Coding With Phoneme-Timbre-Acoustic Representations for Scalable Intelligibility and Fidelity

Jiejie Guo, Student Member, IEEE, Wenjun Xu, Senior Member, IEEE, Fengyu Wang, Member, IEEE,
Junxiao Liang, Student Member, IEEE, JiJie Liu, Jiaqi Wang, Student Member, IEEE, and Ping Zhang, Fellow, IEEE
Abstract: Recent neural speech codecs have demonstrated impressive capabilities in synthesizing high-quality audio from discrete representations. However, prevailing methods rely on frame-level processing, which prevents them from approaching the theoretical compression limit of speech, and lacks semantic scalability to seamlessly transition from intelligibility to high-fidelity within a single model. In this paper, a novel Phoneme-Timbre-Acoustic Neural Speech Codec (PTA-Codec) is proposed, achieving both intelligible and high-fidelity reconstruction across a wide range of bitrates. In specific, first, a semantic-guided hierarchical disentanglement framework is designed, decomposing speech into a three-level representation, i.e., a phoneme layer capturing core linguistic units, a timbre layer modeling slow-varying timbre characteristics, and an acoustic layer reconstructing high-frequency residual details. Further, a phoneme-structure-assisted dynamic compression mechanism is introduced, where phoneme boundaries are incorporated as structural priors to significantly compress frame-level representations into phoneme-level units, drastically reducing bitrate. Extensive experiments demonstrate that PTA-Codec achieves superior performance in the ultra-low bitrate regime, pushing the minimum achievable bitrate down to around 60 bps with a 41.18% absolute reduction in Word Error Rate (WER), while seamlessly delivering high-fidelity reconstruction at up to around 6 kbps with a UTMOS of 4.09.

Experimental Results

We compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art speech codecs from three categories. For traditional codecs, we employ MELPe​ (Link) and Opus​ (Link). For single-rate neural codecs, we implement HIFI-Codec​ (GitHub), BigCodec​ (GitHub), U-Codec​ (GitHub), and LLM-Codec​ (GitHub). For multi-rate neural codecs, we include EnCodec​ (GitHub), WavTokenizer​ (GitHub), Mimi​ (GitHub), SpeechTokenizer​ (GitHub), SemantiCodec​ (GitHub), FlexiCodec​ (GitHub), TiCodec​ (GitHub), and DAC​ (GitHub). Each baseline is evaluated at its representative bitrate(s). To establish fair baselines for exploring lower token rates, we selected DAC and TiCodec as base models and retrained them (denoted as DAC†​ and TiCodec†; see paper for details) on the LibriSpeech train-clean-360 dataset.

We select a subset of audio samples from datasets, presenting the original audio alongside reconstructions from various methods. Below each reconstructed audio, we display the recognized text obtained using the hubert-large-ls960-ft ASR model (HuggingFace).
